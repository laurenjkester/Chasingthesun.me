01

The Clarity Trap

The Witness Reliability Problem

You think you're crystal clear. They heard something completely different. Just like eyewitness testimony, communication degrades instantly. Memory reconstructs. Context colors interpretation. The same sentence lands in five different brains in five different ways.

02

The Intention Trap

The Fundamental Attribution Error

You assume their behavior reveals their intention. It doesn't. We judge ourselves by our intentions, others by their actions and we're almost always wrong about their motives. What you're interpreting as 'resistance' is actually their Stress Behavior.

03

The Reality Trap

Confirmation Bias + Cognitive Dissonance

You believe your view of the situation is 'the truth.' So does everyone else on your team. You can't all be right. Under pressure, confirmation bias intensifies. You see evidence that supports your view and dismiss everything else.
